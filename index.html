Biography

About Me

My name is Frances Staples, though most know me as Frankie. I am a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences, UCLA. Prior to this I obtained my PhD in space plasma physics from the Mullard Space Science Laboratory, University College London.

My research focuses unravelling the mysteries of the near-Earth space environment, also called space weather. I am fascinated with this topic because of its global relevance; space weather is not only the spectacuar auroral light show seen in arctic regions, it imacts modern technologies suhc as satellite communications, GPS, radio signals, and power grids on Earth. Understanding and predicting these space weather can prevent widespread disruptions to people's lives.

Today, I focus on understanding the dynamics of the Van Allen belts, using advanced models and satellite observations to predict radiation exposure to space based infrastructure. My goal is to enhance our ability to safeguard satellites and astronauts from these powerful natural phenomena while sharing my love for space science through public outreach and education.
